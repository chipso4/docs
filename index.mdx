<Note>
**This is a reverse integration guide.** It defines the API endpoints your team will build and host so that Continuum can connect to your systems. Think of it as the contract between us — we handle the consumer experience, and your middleware provides the data.
</Note>

## The 30-second version

Continuum runs the consumer-facing warranty portal for your products. Consumers register, check coverage, and file claims through our UI. Behind the scenes, we call **your** middleware API to retrieve serial data, warranty entitlements, parts, and documents from your systems.

You build the middleware. We handle the rest.

```
Consumer → Continuum → Your API → Your ERP
```

## Getting started

Your middleware is a JSON REST API that translates between Continuum's standard contract and your internal systems. It doesn't matter if you run SAP, Oracle, NetSuite, Epicor, or a custom database — your middleware abstracts that behind a consistent interface.

    - Contractor directory and distributor data
    - Labor rate schedules
    - Registration write-back to your warranty database
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## The workflows your API powers

Every call Continuum makes to your middleware happens within one of these workflows. Each page shows the sequence of API calls, what data flows, and which endpoints are involved.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Product registration" icon="clipboard-check" defaultOpen>
    A consumer enters a serial number → Continuum validates it against your ERP, auto-fills product and distributor fields, and syncs the completed registration back to your warranty database.

    **Your endpoints called:** `validate-unit` → `units/{serial}` → `units/{serial}/distributor` → `contractors` → `registrations/sync`

    [View full flow →](/process-flows/registration)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Warranty verification" icon="shield-check">
    Anyone looks up a serial number → Continuum retrieves unit details and displays warranty coverage broken out by tier (tank, parts, labor), with replacement parts and product documentation.

    **Your endpoints called:** `units/{serial}` → `warranty-coverages` → `units/{serial}/parts` → `media`

    [View full flow →](/process-flows/warranty-verification)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Claims processing" icon="file-invoice">
    A contractor diagnoses a failure → Continuum checks entitlement, presents the BOM for part selection, retrieves warranty codes for failure classification, and finds replacement parts — then manages the claim lifecycle internally.

    **Your endpoints called:** `validate-unit` → `entitlement-information` → `bill-of-materials` → `warranty-codes` → `replacements`

    [View full flow →](/process-flows/claims-processing)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Media center" icon="folder-open">
    A consumer or contractor searches for product documentation → Continuum retrieves manuals, spec sheets, wiring diagrams, images, and other media from your systems via a unified media endpoint.

    **Your endpoints called:** `media?model_number={model}` or `media?part_number={part}`

    [View full flow →](/process-flows/media-center)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Historical import" icon="database">
    On go-live, Continuum pulls your full registration history via paginated bulk export so the portal starts with complete data. After initial load, incremental syncs keep everything current.

    **Your endpoints called:** `import/registrations?page=1&page_size=100`

    [View full flow →](/process-flows/historical-import)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Why this architecture?

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="For your IT team">
    You own the middleware. That means you control security, data mapping, performance, and deployment. Continuum never has direct access to your ERP or databases. You can filter, transform, or redact any field before it leaves your network.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="For your business">
    Consumers get a modern, branded warranty portal without you building one from scratch. Claims that used to take 20+ minutes of manual data entry now process automatically. Contractors stop calling your 1-800 number to check serial numbers.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="For your customers">
    Homeowners register products in minutes instead of mailing in cards. Contractors file warranty claims on-site instead of driving to the distributor with paperwork. Everyone can check coverage status instantly, 24/7.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
