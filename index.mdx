## The 30-second version

Continuum is a warranty and returns automation platform. We manage warranty claims, product returns, and replacement orders on behalf of distributors and manufacturers. Behind the scenes, we call **your** API to look up data and create transactions in your systems.

You build the middleware. We handle the rest.

```
User → Continuum → Your API → Your ERP / OEM Systems
```

---

## Three integration hubs

Continuum's integration surface is organized into three hubs. Each hub serves a different business process. You may use one, two, or all three depending on your needs.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Returns Hub" icon="rotate-left" href="/returns-hub/introduction">
    **Non-defect product returns.** Wrong item shipped, overstock, buyer's remorse. Full RMA lifecycle from creation through receiving and credit.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Warranty Hub" icon="shield-check" href="/warranty-hub/introduction">
    **Warranty replacements and vendor returns.** Create replacement sales orders, manage the return of defective units back to the OEM.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Warranty Claims Hub" icon="file-invoice" href="/warranty-claims-hub/introduction">
    **OEM-facing claim processing.** Validate serial numbers, check entitlement, submit claims to the manufacturer, track through to payment.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How the hubs relate

The three hubs are not completely independent — in a warranty event they are phases in a single orchestrated process. But each hub can also operate standalone.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/architecture-diagram.svg" alt="Integration architecture showing the three hubs and their relationships." />
</Frame>

**The Returns Hub is the foundation.** Customer, product, and invoice lookup endpoints are built here and reused by the Warranty Hub. Start here.

**The Warranty Hub adds replacement orders and vendor returns.** It orchestrates the full warranty event across all three hubs — from identifying the defect through crediting the customer.

**The Warranty Claims Hub connects to the OEM.** It talks to the manufacturer's warranty system (e.g., Rheem, Daikin), not your ERP. It handles serial validation, BOM lookups, and claim submission.
