## What this hub does

The Warranty Hub handles two workflows that sit on top of the Returns Hub foundation:

1. **Replacement orders** — When a defective part is identified, Continuum creates a sales order in your ERP for the replacement. The part is typically picked and handed to the customer at the counter.

2. **Vendor returns** — The defective part needs to go back to the OEM/manufacturer (or be scrapped in field). Continuum creates a vendor return in your ERP to manage that reverse logistics flow.

## How it fits in the warranty event

A warranty event is an end-to-end process across multiple systems. Your ERP integration handles Phases 0, 2, 4, and 5. Phases 1 and 3 happen between Continuum and the OEM — you don't build those.

| Phase | What happens | Who builds it |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **0. Invoice caching** | Continuum bulk-loads invoices from your ERP | **You** (endpoints 1–2 above) |
| 1. Identify the defect | Continuum validates the serial and retrieves the BOM from the OEM | *OEM — not your scope* |
| **2. Replace the part** | Continuum creates a replacement sales order in your ERP, polls until invoiced | **You** (endpoints 5–9 above) |
| 3. File the warranty claim | Continuum submits the claim to the OEM with the invoice details | *OEM — not your scope* |
| **4. Handle defective part** | Continuum creates a vendor return in your ERP (or records scrap) | **You** (endpoints 10–14 above) |
| **5. Credit the customer** | Continuum finalizes the RMA and issues a credit that offsets the replacement order | **You** (endpoints 15–18 above) |

> The two gates between phases: the replacement order must be **invoiced** before the claim is filed (Phase 2 → 3), and the claim must be **approved** before the vendor return and credit are processed (Phase 3 → 4/5). Both gates are managed by Continuum — your ERP just needs to respond to the API calls.
